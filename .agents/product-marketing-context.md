# Product Marketing Context — BrickSuper

> Last updated: 2026-04-01

---

## 1. Product Overview

- **One-liner:** BrickSuper.com.au helps Australian mum and dad investors understand the rules, costs, and risks of buying property through their SMSF — so they can ask better questions when they talk to a licensed adviser.
- **Category:** SMSF property education & lead generation platform
- **Type:** B2C2B — free educational content for consumers, monetised via qualified lead referrals to licensed SMSF advisers
- **Business model:** Flat cost-per-lead (CPL) charged to partner advisory firms. No commission, no trailing fees, no volume-linked remuneration (s963L compliant).
- **Website:** https://bricksuper.com.au
- **Market:** Australia only

---

## 2. Target Audience

### Company/Segment Types
Not applicable (B2C). Audience is individual Australian investors, typically:
- Self-employed tradespeople who own their workshop or premises
- GPs, dentists, accountants renting clinic/office space for 10+ years
- Small business owners (couples) who've built a business from scratch
- PAYG employees with super balance >$200K exploring SMSF

### Decision-Maker Profile
- **Age:** 45–60 (median age of new SMSF members: 46)
- **Household income:** $150K+
- **Super balance:** $200K–$1M+
- **Mindset:** Builders by nature — understand that anything worth having takes time, effort, and the right foundations. Not financial experts, not trying to be. Want it explained straight.
- **Location:** Australia-wide, with partner coverage by state (NSW, QLD, VIC, etc.)

### Primary Use Cases
1. Researching whether an SMSF is right for them before committing
2. Understanding LRBA borrowing structures and current lending rates
3. Assessing whether their business premises qualifies as Business Real Property (BRP)
4. Checking concentration risk and diversification before purchasing
5. Getting connected with a licensed SMSF specialist for personalised advice

## 3. Personas

### Persona 1: "The Tradie" (Primary)
- Owns workshop/premises, renting it to their own business
- Heard mates bought property through super, wants to understand the rules
- Concerned about costs and complexity — doesn't want to be ripped off
- **Motivation:** "I've paid rent on this workshop for 15 years. Could my super own it instead?"
- **Barrier:** Doesn't trust "advisers" pushing products; wants to understand before committing

### Persona 2: "The Professional" (Primary)
- GP, dentist, or accountant renting clinic/office space
- Higher super balance ($500K+), more financially literate
- Wants the numbers — stress tests, concentration analysis, tax implications
- **Motivation:** "I know enough to know I need proper advice, but I want to understand the landscape first."
- **Barrier:** Time-poor, needs information presented efficiently

### Persona 3: "The Couple" (Secondary)
- Partners running a small business together, wondering if they're doing enough with super
- One is more financially engaged than the other — needs content that works for both
- **Motivation:** "We've built this business from nothing. Are we building our retirement properly?"
- **Barrier:** Overwhelmed by jargon; worried about making a mistake with their life savings

## 4. Problems & Pain Points

### Core Challenges
- **No plain-English SMSF property education exists** — everything is either jargon-heavy or sales-driven
- **Post-ASIC crackdown (26-029MR, Feb 2026):** Consumers burned by dodgy lead gen operators using pressure tactics; trust is low
- **Concentration risk not understood** — ATO wrote to 17,700 funds in 2019 about single-asset concentration
- **LRBA complexity** — bare trusts, safe harbour rates (8.85% 2025-26), limited lender market
- **BRP qualification confusion** — the "wholly and exclusively" test is poorly understood
- **Cost underestimation** — setup, acquisition, ongoing annual costs often surprise first-timers

### Emotional Dimensions
- Fear of making an irreversible mistake with their life savings
- Distrust of the advice industry after seeing mates get burned
- Frustration that nobody will explain it straight without trying to sell something
- Pride — they've built everything themselves and don't want to be talked down to

## 7. Objections & Anti-Personas

### Common Objections
- "Why should I trust a website that's funded by advisers?" → Full funding transparency page; flat fee disclosed; no advice given
- "Is this just another spruiker?" → No AFSL, no product sales, no urgency tactics, ASIC-compliant content
- "Can't I just Google this?" → Try finding a plain-English BRP guide or LRBA stress-test tool. We built what doesn't exist.

### Anti-Personas (Poor Fit)
- People looking for get-rich-quick property schemes
- Super balance under $200K without strong contribution trajectory (SMSF costs likely outweigh benefits)
- People wanting specific financial advice (we don't provide this — licensed advisers do)
- Day-traders or speculative investors (SMSF property is a foundation asset, not speculation)

## 8. Switching Dynamics (Four Forces)

### Push (away from status quo)
- Realising rent paid on business premises could have been building equity in super
- ASIC crackdowns making existing lead gen sources untrustworthy
- Mates buying property through SMSF — FOMO but also genuine interest

### Pull (toward BrickSuper)
- Plain-English education that respects intelligence
- Interactive tools that give personalised (but not personal advice) readiness assessment
- Transparent, no-pressure path to connecting with a licensed specialist

### Habit (keeping them where they are)
- "My super is fine where it is" — inertia
- "It's too complicated" — perceived complexity barrier
- Existing relationship with accountant/adviser who hasn't raised SMSF property

### Anxiety (about switching)
- "What if I make a mistake with my retirement savings?"
- "What if the adviser they connect me with is dodgy?"
- "What if SMSF costs eat into my returns?"

## 10. Brand Voice

### Tone
Warm, plain-spoken, balanced, grounded, respectful. "Built on substance, not sales pressure."

### Core Metaphor
**Brick by brick** — property is a foundation asset, not a get-rich-quick instrument. Each decision is a brick, mortared in properly before the next goes down.

### Style Rules
- No jargon without explanation. No acronyms without expansion.
- Every benefit paired with its corresponding risk.
- Cite regulatory sources (ATO, ASIC, SIS Act section numbers).
- Treat the reader as a capable adult in control of their own future.
- **Kitchen table test:** Would a couple sitting at their kitchen table on a Sunday evening understand this without Googling anything?

### Owned Language
- "Laying the foundations" / "brick by brick" / "building something that lasts"
- "Getting the ground right before you build"
- "Bringing in the right tradesperson for the job" (seeking licensed advice)
- "Pouring the slab before the plans are done" (rushing into property)

### Personality
Like a trusted friend who happens to know about SMSFs. Direct, warm, honest — will tell you when the ground isn't ready, even if that's not what you want to hear.

## ⚠️ Compliance Constraints (CRITICAL for All Ad Creative)

**BrickSuper does NOT hold an AFSL. All content must stay within the factual information safe harbour.**

### Banned Phrases (Never Use in Any Ad Copy)
- "independent", "impartial", "unbiased" (s923A restricted)
- "you should", "we recommend" (crosses into financial advice)
- "your super is underperforming" (ASIC red-flag)
- "free super health check" (ASIC enforcement target)
- "high returns", "guaranteed", "risk-free" (misleading)
- "our expert team" (implies in-house advice capacity)
- "turbocharge your super", "unlock your potential" (promotional)
- "get started on your journey" (sales language)
- "act now", "don't wait" (urgency tactics)

### Required in All Ads
- No implied financial advice or personalised recommendation
- No urgency or pressure tactics
- Educational/informational framing only
- If space allows, include or link to disclaimer
- Every claim must be factual and verifiable

### Compliant CTA Examples
- ✅ "Take the free SMSF Property Readiness Quiz"
- ✅ "Read the plain-English LRBA guide"
- ✅ "See how SMSF property borrowing works"
- ✅ "Understand the rules before you talk to an adviser"
- ❌ "Get started on your SMSF property journey today!"
- ❌ "Don't let your super underperform — act now"
