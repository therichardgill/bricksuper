Brand Introduction
Built on substance,
not sales pressure.
BrickSuper is Australia's educational home for SMSF property. Every brand decision — colour, word choice, layout — reflects our commitment to clarity, compliance, and trust. This guide ensures everyone who works with our brand communicates consistently and responsibly.
For creative partners, licensees, content contributors, and internal team members. Questions? Contact the BrickSuper brand team.
01 · Logomark
The Orange Brick
The BrickSuper mark is a stylised brick — solid, structural, and reliable. Its stacked form references both property ownership and the layered nature of superannuation planning. The orange signals warmth and approachability, cutting through the grey formality of financial services.

Clearspace & minimum size

Maintain a minimum clearspace of 1× the height of the brick mark on all sides. Minimum display size: 24px height digital · 12mm print. Never stretch, rotate, recolour, add effects, or separate the mark from its wordmark in public-facing contexts.

Do
Use the reversed (white) version on dark or orange backgrounds. Maintain clearspace on all sides. Use the standalone brick icon only in size-constrained contexts like app icons and favicons. Always pair with wordmark in editorial contexts.
Don't
Apply drop shadows, gradients, or glow effects. Recolour bricks outside the approved palette. Place the logo on busy photography without adequate contrast. Use the wordmark without the brick icon in brand contexts.

Do
Use Serif Display exclusively for page and section titles. Use DM Sans for all UI, body copy, buttons, labels, and legal text. Mix weights intentionally: 300 disclaimers · 400 body · 500 labels · 600 subheads.
Don't
Use serif for body copy, UI labels, or CTAs. Introduce third typefaces. Apply all-caps to headlines. Use font weights above 600. Set legal disclaimers below 11px or in light grey on white.
04 · Voice & Tone
Educator, not salesperson
BrickSuper's voice is grounded, direct, and fair. We explain complexity without dumbing it down. We balance opportunity with risk. We never pressure — we inform. This is not just a brand choice; it is a compliance requirement under ASIC's February 2026 review of SMSF lead generators.
Clear
Plain English over jargon. Define every technical term on first use.
Balanced
Every benefit must be paired with the corresponding risk or caveat.
Grounded
Cite ATO data, legislation, and ASIC guidance. Show your sources.
Respectful
Readers are competent adults making considered financial decisions.
Write this
"An SMSF can hold commercial property — but concentration above 90% in a single asset attracts ATO scrutiny. Your investment strategy must document the rationale in writing."
Factual, balanced, cites a regulatory consequence, directs to documentation obligation.
Not this
"Your super could be working harder! Get started on your SMSF property journey today — our expert team can help you unlock your fund's full potential."
Promotional, implies underperformance, uses restricted framing, implies personalised advice.
Write this
"BrickSuper provides educational information about SMSFs. We can connect you with licensed specialists — but the decision to engage any adviser is always yours."
Transparent about commercial model, preserves user autonomy, legally safe framing.
Not this
"We recommend speaking with one of our independent, unbiased SMSF experts to find the best approach for your situation."
Uses s.923A restricted terms (independent, unbiased), implies capacity for personal advice.
Prohibited words & phrases — ASIC compliance (s.923A & ASIC 26-029MR)
"independent" "impartial" "unbiased" "you should" "we recommend" "the best approach" "your super is underperforming" "free super health check" "high returns" "our expert team" "unlock your potential" "get started on your journey" "we can determine if SMSF is right for you"

07 · Imagery
Real people, real properties
Photography reinforces editorial credibility. We use images that signal professional context — working Australians, authentic commercial spaces, and documentary-style property photography. Never sales-brochure aesthetics.

Orange tint overlays: When applying brand colour to photography in hero sections or social cards, use Brick Orange (#E8611A) at 20–25% opacity with multiply blend mode. Never apply orange overlays to images depicting real people — it reduces accessibility and professionalism.

09 · Compliance Layer
Mandatory disclaimer architecture
Disclaimers are a legal requirement, not a design afterthought. The Westpac v ASIC (2021 HCA 3) decision established that a single general-advice warning is insufficient — disclaimers must be layered, specific, and prominent on every relevant surface. These texts are non-negotiable and must not be abbreviated.
Site footer
BrickSuper.com provides factual educational information only. We do not hold an Australian Financial Services Licence (AFSL) and are not authorised to provide financial product advice. BrickSuper may earn a referral fee when you choose to connect with a licensed adviser — this fee is fixed and does not depend on any financial product you acquire. Always seek advice from a licensed professional before making superannuation or investment decisions.
Tool pages
This tool produces illustrative calculations based on your inputs and general assumptions. It is not financial advice and does not consider your personal circumstances, objectives, or financial situation. Outputs are educational only. Seek advice from a licensed financial adviser before making investment decisions.
Lead forms
By submitting this form, you agree to be contacted by a licensed SMSF specialist. BrickSuper may receive a fixed referral fee from the adviser's licensee. This fee is the same regardless of what advice you receive or whether you proceed with any financial product.
For downloadable lead magnets: every PDF and DOCX must carry a first-page disclaimer and a repeated footer reminder on each page. CTAs within lead magnets must say "Connect with a licensed SMSF specialist" — never "We recommend" or "Based on your situation, you should."
